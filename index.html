About This Work

As a journalist, I'm drawn to storytelling above everything else. When I discovered PhotoShop, InDesign and Figma, I immediately fell in love because they were new tools to bring important stories to life.

This page is a curated sample of work that represents my artistic, creative and technical range. Each piece is something I worked on from start to finish, but I couldn't have made them without the support of so many special people.

The zines are from my time as Magazine Editor at the Daily Trojan. I built every layout by myself, but each one is packed with beautiful illustrations and comics from many other talented artists.

The infographics are from my three years at TACO Inc., an overdose prevention nonprofit where I eventually became the Director of Content Design. My predecessor, Annie, took me in as her intern and introduced me to Figma. She was the one developed the original templates behind the series featuring the carfentanil/shrooms infographics. I created these new editions to these series within Annie's framework, but the foundational thinking was hers, and that's the part of the process that matters the most.

I'm eternally grateful to all of the unbelievably talented people I've been able to collaborate with, and I will always credit them for helping me make the pieces I'm most proud to feature in my portfolio. I owe them so much and I still see their impact in everything I make today.
